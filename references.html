how we did it

Concept
Technology and creativity

A combination of PHP, PostgreSQL, Perl, Apache, HTML, Javascript, jQuery, Twitter Bootstrap brought this site to fruition. Our web app implements these technologies to bring the informed opinion to the user. Our historical analysis provides insight which forecasts lack. When planning a trip far in advance, a weather forecast does little. A macro weather trend provides a general description, suggestive of future weather for a particular date.

The Data
Where did it come from?

Weather Underground provdes an API free of charge to students. Our team of programmers utilized the Perl scripting language to retrieve this information as json data. Once the information was stored and organized, we used PHP to extract and insert the information into a database. Snow base data was provided by Kenny Chan, a website developer. He gave us permission to use his data, which was orginally drawn from another weather site's API. Kenny Chan created a page graphing the information. His data, stored in json, mirrored much the same process we use to insert the weather underground data. During the aforementioned tasks, the characteristics of the data became apparent. Our storage and archival strategy required us to interpret the data per the web app's requirements.

The Design
How does it work?

Based upon our personal experiences and upon observations gathered from web resources, we compiled a scoring system based upon weather conditions for a specified time frame. From weather conditions, such as temperature and snow base, we developed a metric to compare ski resorts. This measurement reflects the 'performance' of each ski resort over one small time span going back five years ( early 2013 - late 2007 ). We believe our approach, though not academically rigorous, provides the insight similar to that of a local resident.
